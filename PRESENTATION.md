# Running Notes demo video

Target duration: **2:50–2:52**. Do not exceed **2:55**, leaving a safety margin below
the three-minute limit.

## 0:00–0:20 — Problem and pitch

**Shown:** Running Notes landing page.

**Narration:**

> Good ideas often come while walking, cooking, or between meetings. We can
> record them quickly, but then forget them. Running Notes puts every recording
> in a separate inbox that you can open in Outlook, where many business people
> already organize their work.

## 0:20–0:30 — Enter the product

**Action:** Click **Try without registering**.

**Shown:** Guest privacy warning and Record tab.

**Narration:**

> This is the live app. Anyone can try the shared guest account without signing
> up.

## 0:30–0:50 — Record a real thought

**Shown:** Record tab.

**Narration before recording:**

> I will record a thought while I am away from my desk.

**Action:** Start recording and say:

> Remember to send Maria the revised project proposal tomorrow morning.

Stop the recording.

## 0:50–1:18 — Receive the note in Thunderbird

**Action:** Switch to Thunderbird, check for new mail, open the new message, and
play the audio file.

**Narration:**

> Now I open Thunderbird and check for new mail. My recording arrives as an
> audio file in a real IMAP mailbox.

Play enough of the attachment for the audience to recognize the recorded
sentence, then continue:

> The app is still simple, and I will make it easier to use later. But the full
> workflow already works in a standard mail app.

## 1:18–2:23 — Codex and GPT-5.6

**Shown:** GitHub repository, `BUILD_WEEK.md`, and one or two relevant pull
requests.

**Narration:**

> Before Build Week, Running Notes was a simple demo for one user. It worked only

> With Codex and GPT-5.6 Sol, I completed the hard production and DevOps work in

**Action:** Open the completed concrete debugging example in `BUILD_WEEK.md`,
then show the relevant pull request, commit, or Codex session.

**Narration:**

> Here is one example. The app only said, "Messages unavailable." Sol found two

## Recording checklist

- Configure the Running Notes account in Thunderbird before recording.
- Clear or identify the Thunderbird inbox so the new message is immediately visible.
- Allow browser microphone access before recording.
- Increase browser and editor zoom so text remains readable at 1080p.
- Hide notifications, bookmarks, credentials, and unrelated tabs.
- Rehearse recording, checking for mail, delivery time, and audio playback.
- Prepare a backup recording in case the live upload fails.
- Edit out loading and waiting time.
- Use no background music unless it is original or appropriately licensed.
- Keep narration clear and the exported video below three minutes.
- Upload the final video publicly to YouTube.
